## Skill Reference
### The Seven Sweeps Framework

**Sweep 1: Clarity**
Check comprehension speed. Can prospects understand the core message in under 5 seconds?

**Weak:** "Our innovative platform leverages cutting-edge algorithms to optimize your workflow efficiency."
**Strong:** "Cut project completion time by 40% with automated task routing."
**Why:** Specific benefit beats feature description. Measurable outcome over vague promise.

**Sweep 2: Voice & Tone**
Eliminate personality shifts within copy blocks.

**Bad:** "Hey there! Are you tired of complex enterprise solutions that require extensive training?"
**Good:** "Skip the 6-month implementation. Start automating tasks today."
**Why:** Consistent professional tone. No fake enthusiasm followed by serious claims.

**Sweep 3: Flow**
Check logical progression and transition smoothness.

**Weak:** "Save time. Reduce errors. Our software integrates with 50+ tools. Start your free trial."
**Strong:** "Save 10 hours weekly → Eliminate manual errors → Integrate your existing tools → Start free trial"
**Why:** Logical progression from problem to outcome to proof to action.

**Sweep 4: Impact**
Maximize emotional and logical punch per word.

**Weak:** "Join thousands of satisfied customers who trust our solution"
**Strong:** "Join 2,847 teams who've eliminated weekend work"
**Why:** Specific number + concrete outcome beats vague social proof.

**Sweep 5: Brevity**
Cut ruthlessly without losing meaning.

**Weak:** "In order to help you achieve better results"
**Strong:** "To improve results"
**Why:** 5 words → 3 words, same meaning.

**Sweep 6: Accuracy**
Verify every claim, stat, and promise.

**Bad:** "Industry-leading performance"
**Good:** "25% faster than Competitor X (benchmark study)"
**Why:** Specific, verifiable claim with proof source.

**Sweep 7: Polish**
Final grammar, spelling, formatting pass.

### Conversion Optimization Checklist

**Value Proposition Clarity (Highest Impact)**
- 5-second test: What does this do for me?
- Benefit hierarchy: Primary benefit above the fold
- Differentiation: Why this vs. alternatives?

**CTA Optimization**
**Weak:** "Submit," "Sign Up," "Learn More"
**Strong:** "Start Free Trial," "Get My Analysis," "See Pricing"
**Why:** Action-specific language reduces cognitive load and sets expectations.

**Social Proof Specificity**
**Weak:** "Trusted by thousands"
**Strong:** "Used by 127 Y Combinator startups"
**Why:** Specific audience creates relevance and credibility.

### Anti-Patterns That Kill Conversion

**The Feature Laundry List**
Lists features without connecting to outcomes. Prospects can't translate features into personal value.

**The Hedge Sandwich**
Surrounding claims with qualifiers: "We might be able to help you potentially improve..." Destroys confidence.

**The Assumption Gap**
Writing for people who already understand your category. New prospects get lost immediately.

**The Cleverness Trap**
Wordplay over clarity. Puns and metaphors that require explanation slow comprehension.

### Testing Recommendations Framework

**Low-Risk Tests (implement directly):**
- CTA button copy variations
- Headline benefit clarity improvements
- Feature → benefit translations

**Medium-Risk Tests (A/B test):**
- Voice and tone shifts
- Value proposition reordering
- Social proof specificity changes

**High-Risk Tests (multivariate testing):**
- Complete messaging pivots
- Audience assumption changes
- Category positioning shifts

**Success Metrics:**
- Comprehension speed (time to understanding)
- Click-through rates on CTAs
- Form completion rates
- Bounce rate improvements
