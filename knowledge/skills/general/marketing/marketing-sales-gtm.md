# Sales & GTM Operations

## Core Frameworks

### Revenue Operations (RevOps)
**Lead Lifecycle Framework:**
1. **Lead Scoring**: Behavioral + demographic + firmographic
2. **Lead Routing**: Territory, product fit, deal size thresholds
3. **Stage Gates**: Clear progression criteria (MQL → SQL → Opportunity)
4. **Handoff Process**: SLA, context transfer, follow-up cadence

**Pipeline Management:**
- Stage definitions with entry/exit criteria
- Velocity tracking by source, rep, segment
- Data hygiene automation and alerts
- Forecast accuracy measurement

### Sales Enablement Assets

**Pitch Deck Structure:**
1. Problem (customer's world without you)
2. Solution (your unique approach)
3. Proof (social proof, results, demos)
4. Proposal (next steps, pricing, timeline)

**One-Pager Framework:**
- Hero statement (value prop in 10 words)
- Three key benefits with metrics
- Social proof (logos, testimonials)
- Clear CTA and contact info

**Battle Card Elements:**
- Competitor overview and positioning
- Feature comparison (head-to-head)
- Pricing comparison and value gaps
- Win/loss talking points
- Objection handling scripts

### Product Launch Strategy

**Launch Sequence (4-week framework):**
- Week -4: Internal enablement, beta feedback
- Week -2: Press/analyst briefings, influencer outreach
- Week 0: Public announcement, customer communications
- Week +2: Campaign amplification, user activation

**Launch Channels Priority:**
1. Existing customers (highest conversion)
2. Email list (owned audience)
3. Product Hunt / industry platforms
4. Social media and PR
5. Paid acquisition

### Pricing Strategy Models

**Value Metric Selection:**
- Align with customer value realization
- Scale with usage/success
- Simple to understand and predict
- Defendable competitive moat

**Packaging Framework:**
- Good/Better/Best structure
- Clear upgrade path and differentiation
- Anchor pricing (premium tier sets context)
- Remove/reduce friction for trial-to-paid

**Competitive Pricing Analysis:**
- Direct feature comparison matrix
- Total cost of ownership (TCO) analysis
- Value-based pricing vs. cost-plus
- Win/loss price sensitivity data

### Competitive Positioning

**Positioning Framework:**
1. **Category**: What market are you in?
2. **Target**: Who specifically benefits most?
3. **Problem**: What pain do you uniquely solve?
4. **Differentiation**: How are you uniquely better?
5. **Proof**: What evidence supports your claims?

**Competitive Intelligence:**
- Feature/capability comparison
- Pricing and packaging analysis
- Customer review sentiment analysis
- Sales cycle and deal size benchmarks
- Messaging and positioning evolution

## Common Workflows

### Creating Sales Decks
1. Gather context (audience, deal stage, key stakeholders)
2. Customize problem/solution fit for specific use case
3. Include relevant proof points and social proof
4. Create clear ask and next steps
5. Provide speaker notes and objection handling

### RevOps Process Design
1. Map current lead journey and identify gaps
2. Define stage criteria and SLA requirements
3. Design automation rules and routing logic
4. Create reporting dashboard and KPIs
5. Build change management and training plan

### Launch Planning
1. Assess market timing and competitive landscape
2. Define success metrics and measurement plan
3. Sequence announcement across channels
4. Create supporting collateral and messaging
5. Plan post-launch amplification and optimization

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- **Feature-focused selling** (vs. outcome-focused)
- **One-size-fits-all** sales materials
- **Launch and abandon** (no post-launch optimization)
- **Cost-plus pricing** without value consideration
- **Generic competitive** positioning
- **RevOps complexity** without clear business impact

## Success Metrics

**Sales Enablement:**
- Asset usage rates by sales team
- Deal velocity improvement
- Win rate by material usage
- Sales feedback and adoption scores

**RevOps:**
- Lead-to-opportunity conversion rates
- Sales cycle length reduction
- Pipeline quality and accuracy
- Marketing-sales alignment scores

**Launch Success:**
- Day 1, Week 1, Month 1 adoption rates
- Press and social mention volume
- Trial-to-paid conversion improvement
- Revenue attribution to launch activities

This framework ensures all GTM activities drive measurable revenue impact and competitive advantage.
