# Conversion Copywriting

## Core Principles

### Clarity Over Cleverness
- Use simple, direct language that anyone can understand
- Avoid jargon, puns, and wordplay that obscure meaning
- One idea per sentence, one theme per paragraph
- Test: Can a distracted prospect understand this in 5 seconds?

### Benefits Over Features
- Lead with what customers gain, not what the product has
- Transform features into specific outcomes
- Use the "So what?" test — every feature should answer why it matters
- Frame benefits in customer's own words and priorities

### Specificity Over Vagueness
- Replace abstract claims with concrete details
- Use numbers, percentages, and measurable outcomes
- Include specific timeframes ("in 30 days" vs "quickly")
- Provide social proof with real names and companies when possible

### Customer Language
- Mirror how your audience actually talks about their problems
- Use their terminology, not internal company language
- Reference their specific pain points and desired outcomes
- Avoid corporate speak and marketing buzzwords

## Content Frameworks

### PAS (Problem-Agitation-Solution)
1. **Problem**: Identify the specific pain point
2. **Agitation**: Amplify the cost of inaction
3. **Solution**: Present your offering as the resolution

### AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
1. **Attention**: Hook with compelling headline
2. **Interest**: Build engagement with relevant benefits
3. **Desire**: Create emotional connection to outcomes
4. **Action**: Clear, specific call-to-action

### Before/After Bridge
1. **Before State**: Current frustrating situation
2. **After State**: Desired outcome
3. **Bridge**: Your solution as the path between them

## Content Type Workflows

### Website Copy
1. **Research Phase**
   - Customer interviews and feedback analysis
   - Competitor messaging audit
   - Performance data from existing pages
   - SEO keyword research and search intent

2. **Structure Phase**
   - Headline: Primary benefit in customer language
   - Subheadline: Specific outcome or proof point
   - Body: Benefits in order of importance
   - Social proof: Testimonials, case studies, logos
   - CTA: Clear next step with urgency

3. **Optimization Phase**
   - A/B test headlines and CTAs
   - Monitor conversion rates and user behavior
   - Refine based on customer feedback

### Email Campaigns
1. **Subject Line**: Curiosity + benefit, avoid spam triggers
2. **Opening**: Personal hook or pattern interrupt
3. **Body**: One clear message with specific benefit
4. **CTA**: Single, prominent action with urgency
5. **P.S.**: Reinforce key benefit or add urgency

### Social Media Content
1. **Hook**: First line must stop the scroll
2. **Value**: Educational, entertaining, or inspiring content
3. **Proof**: Social validation or credibility marker
4. **Action**: Clear next step or engagement prompt

### Cold Email Sequences
1. **Research**: Specific company/person insights
2. **Personalization**: Relevant observation or compliment
3. **Value Proposition**: Specific benefit for their situation
4. **Soft CTA**: Low-friction next step
5. **Follow-up**: New angle or additional value

## Quality Checkpoints

### Pre-Publication Checklist
- [ ] Passes the 5-second clarity test
- [ ] Benefits clearly outweigh feature mentions 3:1
- [ ] Includes specific numbers or outcomes
- [ ] Uses customer language, not corporate speak
- [ ] Has single, clear call-to-action
- [ ] Mobile-friendly formatting
- [ ] Spell-check and grammar review

### Performance Indicators
- **Email**: Open rate >25%, click rate >3%
- **Social**: Engagement rate >2%, click-through >1%
- **Website**: Time on page >30 seconds, conversion rate improvement
- **Cold Email**: Reply rate >10%, positive response rate >5%

## Common Anti-Patterns

### Avoid These Mistakes
- **Feature dumping**: Listing capabilities without benefits
- **Corporate speak**: "Leverage synergies," "best-in-class solutions"
- **Weak CTAs**: "Learn more," "Click here"
- **Vague benefits**: "Increase efficiency," "Save time"
- **Missing urgency**: No reason to act now vs later
- **Multiple CTAs**: Confusing the primary action

### Red Flag Phrases
- "World-class," "industry-leading," "innovative"
- "Solutions," "platform," "ecosystem"
- "Seamless," "robust," "scalable"
- "Game-changing," "revolutionary," "cutting-edge"

## Brand Voice Consistency

### Voice Audit Questions
1. Does this sound like our brand personality?
2. Would our ideal customer say this?
3. Is the tone appropriate for the channel?
4. Does it align with our brand values?

### Channel Adaptations
- **LinkedIn**: Professional but conversational
- **Twitter**: Concise and engaging
- **Email**: Personal and direct
- **Website**: Authoritative and clear
- **Ads**: Attention-grabbing and benefit-focused
