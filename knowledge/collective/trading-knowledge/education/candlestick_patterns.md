# Complete Candlestick Pattern Encyclopedia

> The validator must recognize these patterns on charts and understand when they're reliable vs noise. A pattern without context is just a shape — always check trend, location (S/R), and volume.

## Pattern Reliability Key
- **HIGH**: Reliable standalone with proper context (trend + S/R level)
- **MEDIUM-HIGH**: Reliable with one confirming indicator (RSI, volume, BB)
- **MEDIUM**: Needs 2+ confirming signals
- **LOW**: Only trade with strong confluence from other sources

## SINGLE-CANDLE PATTERNS

### Hammer (Bullish Reversal)
- **Structure**: Small body at top, long lower shadow (2x+ body length), little/no upper shadow
- **Context**: Must appear after downtrend or at support level
- **Reliability**: HIGH at key support, MEDIUM in random location
- **What it means**: Sellers pushed price down hard but buyers absorbed it and pushed back up — rejection of lower prices
- **Trade**: Enter on next candle's open if it closes above hammer's body. Stop below hammer's low.
- **Fail signal**: Next candle closes below hammer's low = pattern failed

### Inverted Hammer (Bullish Reversal)
- **Structure**: Small body at bottom, long upper shadow, little/no lower shadow
- **Context**: After downtrend. Less reliable than hammer — needs confirmation candle
- **Reliability**: MEDIUM — always wait for bullish confirmation candle
- **What it means**: Buyers tried to push up but couldn't hold. However, selling pressure is weakening.
- **Trade**: Only enter if next candle closes bullish above the inverted hammer's high.

### Hanging Man (Bearish Reversal)
- **Structure**: Identical to hammer but appears after uptrend
- **Context**: Must appear after uptrend or at resistance
- **Reliability**: MEDIUM — needs bearish confirmation candle
- **What it means**: Despite bullish trend, sellers managed a significant push down. Warning sign.
- **Trade**: Enter short if next candle closes below hanging man's body. Stop above high.

### Shooting Star (Bearish Reversal)
- **Structure**: Small body at bottom, long upper shadow (2x+ body), little/no lower shadow
- **Context**: After uptrend or at resistance
- **Reliability**: HIGH at resistance, MEDIUM elsewhere
- **What it means**: Buyers pushed hard but sellers overwhelmed them — strong rejection of higher prices
- **Trade**: Enter short on next candle if it opens below shooting star body. Stop above shadow high.

### Doji (Indecision / Potential Reversal)
- **Structure**: Open and close are equal (or near-equal). Shadow lengths vary by type.
- **Context**: After a strong trend = reversal signal. In consolidation = meaningless noise.
- **Reliability**: MEDIUM — always needs confirmation from next candle

#### Doji Variants:
- **Standard Doji**: Equal shadows top and bottom. Pure indecision.
- **Long-Legged Doji**: Very long shadows both ways. Extreme indecision — big move coming.
- **Dragonfly Doji**: Long lower shadow, no upper shadow. Bullish at support (like a hammer with no body).
- **Gravestone Doji**: Long upper shadow, no lower shadow. Bearish at resistance (like a shooting star with no body).
- **Four-Price Doji**: Open=High=Low=Close. Extremely rare. Complete indecision on very low volume.

### Spinning Top (Indecision)
- **Structure**: Small body, shadows on both sides (shorter than long-legged doji)
- **Context**: In a trend = potential pause or reversal. Needs context.
- **Reliability**: LOW alone — only meaningful in context of surrounding candles
- **What it means**: Neither bulls nor bears in control. Wait for resolution.

### Marubozu (Strong Momentum)
- **Structure**: Long body with NO shadows (or very tiny)
- **Context**: Anywhere — this is a pure momentum signal
- **Reliability**: HIGH for trend continuation
- **Bullish Marubozu**: Opens at low, closes at high. Buyers dominated entire session.
- **Bearish Marubozu**: Opens at high, closes at low. Sellers dominated entire session.
- **Trade**: Strong continuation signal. Enter in direction of marubozu. Watch for exhaustion if after extended move.

## TWO-CANDLE PATTERNS

### Bullish Engulfing (Bullish Reversal)
- **Structure**: Small bearish candle followed by larger bullish candle that completely engulfs it (body covers prior body)
- **Context**: After downtrend or at support. The bigger the engulfing candle relative to prior, the stronger.
- **Reliability**: HIGH — one of the most reliable reversal patterns
- **What it means**: Sellers had control (candle 1), then buyers completely overwhelmed them (candle 2)
- **Trade**: Enter long at close of engulfing candle or next open. Stop below engulfing candle low.
- **Volume**: Should increase on the engulfing candle for highest reliability

### Bearish Engulfing (Bearish Reversal)
- **Structure**: Small bullish candle followed by larger bearish candle that completely engulfs it
- **Context**: After uptrend or at resistance
- **Reliability**: HIGH
- **Trade**: Enter short at close of engulfing candle. Stop above its high.

### Piercing Line (Bullish Reversal)
- **Structure**: Bearish candle, then bullish candle that opens below prior low but closes above the midpoint of prior candle's body
- **Context**: After downtrend. Must close above 50% of prior candle body (the deeper, the better)
- **Reliability**: MEDIUM-HIGH
- **What it means**: Sellers gapped down but buyers fought back past the halfway point — momentum shifting
- **Trade**: Enter long on confirmation (next candle bullish). Stop below the low of the pattern.

### Dark Cloud Cover (Bearish Reversal)
- **Structure**: Bullish candle, then bearish candle that opens above prior high but closes below midpoint of prior body
- **Context**: After uptrend. Mirror of piercing line.
- **Reliability**: MEDIUM-HIGH
- **Trade**: Enter short on confirmation. Stop above pattern high.

### Bullish Harami (Bullish Reversal)
- **Structure**: Large bearish candle followed by small bullish candle contained within prior body
- **Context**: After downtrend. The small candle shows selling pressure has dried up.
- **Reliability**: MEDIUM — weaker than engulfing. Needs confirmation.
- **What it means**: After aggressive selling, a small candle shows balance — sellers are exhausted
- **Trade**: Wait for third candle confirmation before entering. Stop below large candle's low.

### Bearish Harami (Bearish Reversal)
- **Structure**: Large bullish candle followed by small bearish candle contained within prior body
- **Context**: After uptrend. Mirror of bullish harami.
- **Reliability**: MEDIUM
- **Trade**: Wait for confirmation candle.

### Tweezer Bottom (Bullish Reversal)
- **Structure**: Two candles with matching (or near-matching) lows. First bearish, second bullish.
- **Context**: At support level. The matching lows show price was rejected twice at the same level.
- **Reliability**: MEDIUM-HIGH at key support levels
- **What it means**: Price tested the same low twice and was rejected both times — strong floor
- **Trade**: Enter long after second candle closes. Stop below the matching lows.

### Tweezer Top (Bearish Reversal)
- **Structure**: Two candles with matching highs. First bullish, second bearish.
- **Context**: At resistance. Mirror of tweezer bottom.
- **Reliability**: MEDIUM-HIGH at key resistance
- **Trade**: Enter short after second candle. Stop above matching highs.

### Kicking (Strong Reversal)
- **Structure**: Bearish marubozu followed by bullish marubozu that gaps UP (or vice versa for bearish kicking)
- **Context**: Anywhere — this is one of the strongest candlestick signals
- **Reliability**: HIGH — very rare but very powerful
- **What it means**: Complete sentiment reversal. One side had total control, then the other took over with a gap.
- **Trade**: Enter in direction of the second marubozu immediately. Tight stop behind the gap.
- **Note**: Rare on forex M15 due to continuous trading, but appears around news events and session opens.

## THREE-CANDLE PATTERNS

### Morning Star (Bullish Reversal)
- **Structure**: 1) Large bearish candle, 2) Small-bodied candle (star) that gaps down, 3) Large bullish candle that closes above midpoint of candle 1
- **Context**: After downtrend or at significant support
- **Reliability**: HIGH — one of the strongest 3-candle reversal patterns
- **What it means**: Strong selling (1), indecision (2), strong buying (3). Clear sentiment shift.
- **Trade**: Enter long at close of candle 3. Stop below the star's low.
- **Best when**: Star is a doji (morning doji star = even more reliable)

### Evening Star (Bearish Reversal)
- **Structure**: 1) Large bullish, 2) Small-bodied star gapping up, 3) Large bearish closing below midpoint of candle 1
- **Context**: After uptrend or at resistance. Mirror of morning star.
- **Reliability**: HIGH
- **Trade**: Enter short at close of candle 3. Stop above star's high.

### Three White Soldiers (Bullish Continuation/Reversal)
- **Structure**: Three consecutive bullish candles, each opening within prior body and closing higher
- **Context**: After downtrend (reversal) or early in uptrend (confirmation)
- **Reliability**: HIGH for trend establishment
- **What it means**: Sustained buying pressure over three periods — bulls firmly in control
- **Trade**: Enter long on close of third candle. Stop below first candle's low.
- **Warning**: If bodies get progressively smaller = weakening momentum, possible exhaustion

### Three Black Crows (Bearish Continuation/Reversal)
- **Structure**: Three consecutive bearish candles, each opening within prior body and closing lower
- **Context**: After uptrend (reversal) or early in downtrend (confirmation). Mirror of three white soldiers.
- **Reliability**: HIGH
- **Warning**: Same as soldiers — watch for diminishing body sizes = exhaustion

### Three Inside Up (Bullish Reversal)
- **Structure**: 1) Large bearish, 2) Small bullish inside (harami), 3) Bullish candle closing above candle 1's high
- **Context**: After downtrend. This is a confirmed harami — the third candle proves the reversal.
- **Reliability**: MEDIUM-HIGH — more reliable than harami alone because of confirmation
- **Trade**: Enter long on close of candle 3.

### Three Inside Down (Bearish Reversal)
- **Structure**: 1) Large bullish, 2) Small bearish inside, 3) Bearish candle closing below candle 1's low
- **Context**: After uptrend. Mirror of three inside up.
- **Reliability**: MEDIUM-HIGH

### Abandoned Baby (Strong Reversal)
- **Structure**: 1) Candle in trend direction, 2) Doji that GAPS away from candles 1 and 3, 3) Candle in reversal direction
- **Context**: Very rare in forex (requires gaps). Most common at session opens or around major news.
- **Reliability**: HIGH when it appears — but extremely rare on M15 forex
- **What it means**: The doji is "abandoned" — isolated by gaps on both sides. Total sentiment reversal.
- **Trade**: Enter on close of candle 3. Very strong signal.

### Rising Three Methods (Bullish Continuation)
- **Structure**: 1) Long bullish, 2-4) Small bearish candles staying within candle 1's range, 5) Long bullish closing above candle 1's high
- **Context**: During uptrend. The small candles are a rest/consolidation before continuation.
- **Reliability**: MEDIUM-HIGH for continuation
- **What it means**: Brief profit-taking pause within a strong trend, then trend resumes
- **Trade**: Enter long on close of candle 5. Stop below the consolidation low.

### Falling Three Methods (Bearish Continuation)
- **Structure**: Mirror of rising three methods but inverted for downtrend
- **Context**: During downtrend. Mirror pattern.
- **Reliability**: MEDIUM-HIGH

## CONTEXT RULES — WHEN TO TRUST A PATTERN

### Location Matters Most
1. **At key S/R level** → Pattern reliability doubles
2. **At EMA (E55, E100)** → Strong confluence with thesis framework
3. **At Bollinger Band** → Volatility-informed reversal zone
4. **In empty space** → Pattern reliability drops significantly

### Trend Context
1. **Reversal pattern WITH the higher timeframe trend** → Most reliable (e.g., bullish hammer during H4 uptrend pullback)
2. **Reversal pattern AGAINST the higher timeframe trend** → Less reliable, needs more confirmation
3. **Continuation pattern WITH the trend** → High reliability
4. **Pattern in consolidation/range** → Low reliability unless at range boundary

### Volume Confirmation
- Reversal patterns should show INCREASING volume on the reversal candle
- Continuation patterns work even on normal volume
- Patterns on very low volume are noise — ignore them

### Candle Size
- Patterns with larger candles (relative to recent ATR) are more significant
- A hammer with a shadow of 2x ATR is much more significant than one with 0.5x ATR
- Marubozu covering 1.5x+ ATR is a very strong signal

### Multiple Pattern Confluence
- Two patterns at the same level = very strong (e.g., tweezer bottom + bullish engulfing)
- Pattern + indicator signal = strong (e.g., hammer + RSI oversold + at E100)
- Three signals = high conviction trade
